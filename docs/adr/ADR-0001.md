# ADR-0001: SongDNA as the Aggregate Root

**Status:** Accepted  
**Date:** 2026-07-21

---

## Context

EchoInsight models the complete analytical representation of a song.

A single analysis produces multiple related pieces of information:

- Song metadata
- Song-level summary metrics
- Structural organization
- Per-segment DSP features
- Frame-level analysis references
- Analysis provenance

These objects are not independent. They describe different aspects of the same song and must remain internally consistent.

The primary architectural question was:

> Should these objects be modeled as independent aggregates, or should they form a single aggregate with one authoritative root?

The answer affects loading, persistence, validation, serialization, caching, and future extensibility.

---

## Decision Drivers

The decision prioritized the following qualities:

- Domain consistency
- Simple persistence
- Strong invariants
- Predictable loading behaviour
- Ease of reasoning
- Maintainability

---

## Decision

**SongDNA is the sole aggregate root of the domain model.**

```text
SongDNA
├── Metadata
├── Summary
├── StructureDNA
│   └── SegmentDNA[]
├── FrameReference
└── AnalysisManifest
```

`StructureDNA` and `SegmentDNA` are owned by `SongDNA` through composition. They are never loaded, persisted, or manipulated as independent domain aggregates.

Loading a song therefore loads its complete structural representation in a single operation.

This decision reflects the domain itself.

Segments have meaning only within the context of a song. Their ordering, timing, and relationships are defined by the structure of their parent. Treating them as independent entities would require every consumer to reconstruct relationships that are already inherent to the model.

The aggregate therefore becomes the authoritative boundary for validation, serialization, and lifecycle management.

---

## Alternatives Considered

### Independent objects with shared identifiers

Each domain type would exist independently and be linked using `song_id`.

Consumers would assemble the full representation manually.

**Rejected because**

- validation becomes duplicated across consumers
- orphaned or inconsistent references become possible
- consumers must reconstruct ordering and structural relationships
- persistence becomes fragmented across multiple files

This approach shifts responsibility away from the domain model and into application code.

---

### Multiple aggregate roots

`SongDNA`, `StructureDNA`, and `SegmentDNA` would each become top-level aggregates with independent persistence.

**Rejected because**

The relationships between these objects are **containment**, not **association**.

A segment has no meaningful lifecycle outside the structure that owns it.

Introducing multiple aggregates would require cross-aggregate consistency rules without providing any practical benefit for EchoInsight's file-based storage model.

---

## Consequences

### Advantages

- One serialization unit per song
- One loading path for every consumer
- Centralized validation of structural invariants
- Straightforward caching
- Simpler persistence model
- Clear ownership relationships throughout the domain

### Disadvantages

- Loading a song also loads its structural representation.
- Future extensions require careful consideration before becoming part of the aggregate.

### Tradeoffs

For the current storage model, SongDNA aggregates are expected to remain compact enough that loading the complete aggregate is inexpensive.

If future use cases require metadata-only queries across very large collections, those should be addressed through indexing or auxiliary data structures rather than fragmenting the aggregate itself.

---

## Future Considerations

Future analytical artifacts—such as stem analysis, embeddings, or machine-learning outputs—should be evaluated independently rather than automatically becoming part of `SongDNA`.

The guiding principle is:

> Extend the model only when the new information shares the same lifecycle, ownership, and consistency requirements as the aggregate itself.
